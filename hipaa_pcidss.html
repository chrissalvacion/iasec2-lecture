What is HIPAA?

Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act

A U.S. law setting standards for protecting sensitive patient health information, known as Protected Health Information (PHI).

Key Objectives:

  • Ensure patient data confidentiality.
  • Protect against unauthorized access.
  • Maintain data integrity and availability.

The HIPAA Security Rule

The Security Rule mandates three types of safeguards:

1. Administrative

Policies, training, and access management protocols.

2. Physical

Secure facilities, device locks, and restricted workstation access.

3. Technical

Encryption, authentication, and secure audit logs.

Example: Hospitals must limit system access to authorized medical personnel only.

Importance of HIPAA Compliance

Failure to comply is not just a policy violation; it has legal consequences.

  • Heavy Fines: Penalties can range from thousands to millions of dollars.
  • Legal Action: Possibility of criminal charges for negligence.
  • Loss of Trust: Patients lose faith in the provider's ability to keep secrets.

Compliance supports continuous improvement in healthcare security.

What is PCI DSS?

Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard

A global security standard designed to protect credit and debit card information during payment processing.

Who Must Comply?

  • Merchants (Retailers, Online Stores)
  • Banks & Financial Institutions
  • Payment Processors

PCI DSS Requirements (Overview)

Build a Secure Network
Install firewalls and do not use vendor-supplied defaults for passwords.
Protect Data
Protect stored cardholder data and encrypt transmission across open networks.
Vulnerability Mgmt
Use anti-virus software and develop secure systems/applications.
Access Control
Restrict access to data by business need-to-know.
Monitor Networks
Track and monitor all access to network resources and cardholder data.
InfoSec Policy
Maintain a policy that addresses information security for all personnel.

PCI DSS in Action

Example: An online shopping website must encrypt card data, use secure payment gateways, and perform regular vulnerability scans.

Consequences of Non-Compliance

  • Financial penalties and fines.
  • Loss of ability to process card payments (a business stopper).
  • Severe reputation damage.
